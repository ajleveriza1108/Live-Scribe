# Taglish and Code-Switching Notes

Taglish is not an error state. A speaker may switch languages within a sentence for precision, familiarity, emphasis, or normal conversation.

## Preservation rules
- Keep the language actually spoken.
- Do not translate English technical vocabulary merely because the surrounding sentence is Tagalog.
- Preserve particles such as po, naman, kasi, pala, nga, yata, daw/raw, and din/rin.
- Treat borrowed and inflected forms carefully, such as “nag-download,” “mag-meeting,” and “i-submit.”
- Use a custom dictionary for names and organization-specific terminology.
