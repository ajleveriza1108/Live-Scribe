# Tagalog Grammar Review Notes

Tagalog has flexible word order and natural regional variation. The review engine must be conservative.

## Useful distinctions
- “ng” commonly marks an object, possession, amount, or modifier.
- “nang” can mark manner, time, repetition, or connect a modifier to a verb.
- “may” and “mayroon” overlap but are not interchangeable in every construction.
- “din/rin” and “daw/raw” often change according to the preceding sound in formal writing.

Do not auto-correct these forms using a simple replacement table. Context is required. Add a review comment or leave the verbatim wording unchanged.
