LIVE SCRIBE v0.6.2 — BUYER QUICK GUIDE
================================================

WHAT IT DOES
------------
Live Scribe listens to a microphone and creates English, Tagalog/
Filipino, or Taglish text. It saves the original audio as WAV while the
speaker talks. After Stop, it can clean steady background noise, transcribe
the complete WAV again, compare both transcription passes, and prepare a
formatted Word report with review comments.

FIRST START AND MODEL DOWNLOAD
------------------------------
1. Extract the complete ZIP to Documents, Desktop, or a writable portable SSD.
2. Keep every folder together.
3. Open the launcher for your operating system:
   Windows: Start Live Scribe.bat
   macOS:   Start Live Scribe.command
   Linux:   start_linux.sh
4. Permit microphone access when the operating system asks.
5. The app opens without downloading a model.
6. Select a speech model inside the app. Start with small.
7. Click Download Selected Model.
8. Keep the app open and connected until the one-time download finishes.
9. Start Listening becomes available after the model is ready.
10. Later sessions reuse that downloaded model offline.

The portable app dependencies are already included. A model is downloaded only
when you select it and press the download button.

CURRENT LANGUAGE SUPPORT
------------------------
This edition is optimized for English, Tagalog/Filipino, and Taglish. More
languages are planned for future versions, but are not enabled in this edition.

NORMAL SESSION
--------------
1. The app automatically tries the system default microphone.
2. Use Detect if the microphone list changes.
3. Press Start Listening.
4. Speak naturally. Rough text appears after short pauses.
5. Press Stop & Save WAV when the speaker is finished.
6. Keep the app open while it checks the complete WAV.
7. Review the Final Transcript and Review Comments tabs.
8. Save DOCX, TXT, or SRT.

DIFFICULT WORDS AND NAMES
-------------------------
Open Vocabulary & Pronunciation inside the app. Enter:
- the correct written spelling; and
- one or more ways the word may sound or be mistakenly transcribed.

Example:
Correct spelling: Project Lakbay
Sounds like: project lock buy, project lak bai

The app uses these forms as recognition hints. During the final WAV pass, an
exact matched alias may be corrected to the written form and listed in Review
Comments. Use distinctive aliases and avoid very short common words.

IMPORTANT FILES
---------------
recordings/  Original and optional enhanced WAV files.
exports/     Saved Word, text, and subtitle files.
models/      Downloaded speech models. Keep these to avoid downloading again.
dictionary/  Names, special terms, pronunciation aliases, and replacements.
Skills/      Small transcription and review instruction files.
Knowledge/   Compact English, Tagalog, Taglish, and accuracy references.

NOISE AND MICROPHONE TIPS
-------------------------
- Put the microphone near the speaker.
- Avoid music, televisions, echoes, and several people talking together.
- Built-in cleanup is best for steady fan or air-conditioner noise.
- Noise reduction cannot reconstruct words that were not recorded clearly.
- The original WAV is always kept and is the best source for final checking.

ACCURACY NOTICE
---------------
AI-assisted transcription can make mistakes. Always review the final
transcript and replay the saved WAV before relying on names, dates, numbers,
amounts, addresses, quotations, legal/medical wording, uncommon terms, or
other important information. Grammar and diction comments are suggestions,
not proof that the transcription is correct.

PORTABILITY
-----------
Move the complete extracted folder, not only the launcher. Stop the app before
unplugging an external drive. Keep a backup of important recordings, documents,
models, dictionary files, and the original purchased ZIP.


MODEL DOWNLOAD PROGRESS
-----------------------
A progress bar appears automatically after you approve a model download. It
shows the downloaded size, total size, percentage, speed, and approximate time
remaining. Keep Live Scribe open until the bar disappears and the app confirms
that the model is ready.

LIVESTREAM / COMPUTER AUDIO
---------------------------
Choose "Computer audio / livestream" under "Listen to", click Detect, and choose
the speakers or headphones that are playing the stream. Start the livestream
before clicking Start Listening.

Windows captures the selected output directly. Linux requires a PulseAudio or
PipeWire monitor source. macOS requires a virtual audio device such as BlackHole.
The app records the captured stream to WAV and can verify it afterward.

SPEECH QUALITY CHOICES
----------------------
Compact — about 486 MB
    Fastest and smallest. Useful for basic tests and older computers.

Balanced — about 1.53 GB
    Good accuracy, particularly for CPU-only computers.

Best Overall — about 1.62 GB
    Recommended balance of speed and accuracy for most buyers.

Maximum Accuracy — about 3.09 GB
    Highest quality, largest download, and slowest processing.

The same downloaded quality handles English, Filipino/Tagalog, Taglish, Spanish,
French, German, Italian, Portuguese, and Dutch.

APPEARANCE
----------
Use the Appearance selector in the lower-left corner to choose OLED Black or
Dirty White. The preference is saved locally.

STOPPING AND RESUMING A MODEL DOWNLOAD
----------------------------------------
While a speech-quality model is downloading, click Stop Download to pause it safely.
Wait for the progress card to disappear before closing Live Scribe. Partial files
remain in the portable models folder. Choose the same quality and click Download
Selected Quality later to resume from the saved progress.

VOCABULARY MANAGER
------------------
Open Vocabulary Manager to add new entries, select and edit existing entries, or
remove entries that are no longer needed. Changes apply to the next session.

TOPIC PROFILES
--------------
Before starting, choose the closest Topic profile on the Live Session page.
The included profiles cover everyday conversations, office meetings, classes,
Zoom/Google Meet calls, interviews, sermons, livestreams, programming,
e-commerce, and news.

Open Topics in the sidebar to add, edit, or remove profiles. Enter a short
description and important names or terms. The profile is saved locally and can
be reused later. It does not require another LLM or model download.

A Topic Profile is a recognition hint only. Always verify important names,
numbers, dates, quotations, and decisions against the saved WAV.

FIRST-RUN PC CHECK
------------------
Live Scribe checks RAM, CPU threads, free model storage, processor architecture,
and compatible NVIDIA GPU access before offering model downloads. The check is
performed locally. Hardware information is not uploaded.

Speech qualities are marked Recommended, May run slowly, or Unavailable.
Unavailable models are removed from the download selector. A model remains
available with a warning when Live Scribe is not certain that it will fail.

Open Models and click "Check This PC Again" after moving the portable folder to
another computer, adding RAM, freeing storage, or changing GPU setup.

This is a conservative estimate, not a speed or accuracy guarantee. Close
memory-heavy programs during transcription and always verify important content
against the saved WAV.

PORTABLE FLASH-DRIVE USE
------------------------
Keep the whole Live Scribe folder together. Do not copy only the executable.
Use a fast USB 3.x flash drive or portable SSD for the smoothest startup and
model loading.

The app keeps models, settings, recordings, exports, topic profiles, vocabulary,
download cache, and temporary files in the portable folder.

Do not remove the drive while downloading, recording, verifying, exporting, or
saving. Close Live Scribe normally and safely eject the drive afterward.

The first-run PC check includes a small portable-drive speed test. Slow storage
creates a warning but does not automatically disable a model.
